Switching to an electric vehicle (EV) helps reduce greenhouse gas emissions significantly because unlike gas-powered cars, EVs produce no tailpipe emissions, which emit carbon dioxide and other pollutants. See how purchasing an EV has become easier for you!

In addition to policies from the federal government, New Jersey provides many benefits to electric vehicle owners.

Federal State
Purchase Incentive - New Clean Vehicle Credit (Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 (IRA), Section 30D):
    Buyers may qualify for up to $7,500 in tax credits.
    Eligibility depends on vehicle specifications such as weight, assembly location, battery components, and the retail price.
    Personal income caps are also applied on Adjusted Gross Income:
      $300,000 for joint filers or surviving spouses
      $225,000 for heads of households
      $150,000 for single filers
    Vehicle must be used for personal use, not for resale.
    The vehicle must be purchased new.
    The seller must report your name and taxpayer identification number to the IRS after vehicle sale.
    Vehicle requirements:
      Battery capacity of 7 kWh or more
      Gross weight under 14,000 pounds
      Final assembly in North America
      Manufacturer Suggested Retail Price limits:
        $80,000 for vans, SUVs, and pickup trucks
        $55,000 for other vehicles
    Credit Breakdown (Max $7,500):
      Vehicles serviced from Jan. 1 - Apr. 17, 2023
        Base: $2,500
        Additional $417 if at least 7 kWh of battery capacity
        Additional $417 per kWh beyond 5 kWh capacity
      Vehicles serviced from Apr. 18, 2023 and after
        Same requirements as above, in addition to:
        Must meet critical minerals requirement ($3,750), OR
        Must meet battery components requirement ($3,750)
        Vehicles that meet both requirements can receive full $7,500 tax credit

To claim your tax credits, file Form 8936 with your tax return, including your vehicle's VIN and the time-of-sale report which you obtained during purchase from the seller.

For more information, visit the official IRS website. 		Regional Greenhouse Gas Initiative (RGGI)

The RGGI is a cooperative effort among several U.S. states to reduce greenhouse gas emissions through a cap-and-trade program. In New Jersey, RGGI proceeds are strategically invested to support clean energy initiatives, environmental justice, and economic development. Funds from RGGI auctions are allocated by the New Jersey Department of Environmental Protection (NJDEP), the New Jersey Board of Public Utilities (NJBPU), and the New Jersey Economic Development Authority (NJEDA).

New Jersey's RGGI Strategic Funding Plan focuses on six key objectives, including reducing greenhouse gas emissions, promoting energy efficiency, and addressing environmental health in disproportionately impacted communities. Proceeds support programs such as electrifying transportation, promoting clean energy technologies, and improving energy efficiency in buildings, including low-income housing.

  1. It Pay$ to Plug In: A grant program by NJDEP that aims to promote widespread access to EV charging in public and semi-public locations to support the adoption of EVs statewide, combatting range anxiety.

    Eligible Locations

    • Public Parking Lots

    If approved, this grant covers up to 80% of the cost for charging station hardware and installation, depending on the location.

  2. Charge Up New Jersey: An incentive program designed to encourage the adoption of EVs by providing financial rebates to residents who purchase or lease eligible EVs and chargers.

    3. Rebate Amounts: Rebates offer $2,000 for eligible NJ residents under the Charge Up incentive, and an additional $2,000 under the Charge Up+ incentive for income-qualifying applicants, for a maximum of $4,000.

    Eligibility Requirements: Vehicles must have a Manufacturer's Suggested Retail Price below $55,000 and be purchased or leased from participating dealerships within New Jersey. The program is only for individual consumers.

    Application Process: The rebate is applied at the point of sale or lease, with the dealership submitting the application on behalf of the buyer. Individuals must apply online to prequalify for the Charge Up+ additional incentive.

    For more information about purchase incentives under Charge Up New Jersey, visit their website.


    Charging Incentive: Charge Up offers up to $250 for the purchase and installation an eligible Level 2 charger, which adds about 15-35 miles of range per hour of charging time. Visit the Charge Up NJ website for more information.
Purchase Incentive - Used Clean Vehicle Credit (Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 (IRA), Section 25E):
    Buyers may qualify for up to $4,500 or 30% of the purchase price (whichever is less) in tax credits.
    Eligible individuals may not be the original owners or be claimed as a dependent on another person's tax return.
    Personal income caps on Adjusted Gross Income:
      $150,000 for joint filers or surviving spouses
      $112,000 for heads of households
      $75,000 for single filers
    Vehicle must be used for personal use, not for resale.
    Buyer cannot have claimed another used clean vehicle credit in the last three years.
    Vehicle requirements:
      Model year must be at least 2 years old
        e.g. A vehicle purchased in 2024 must be a 2022 model or older
      Battery capacity of 7 kWh or more
      Gross weight under 14,000 pounds
      The purchase price must not exceed $25,000
      Vehicle must be purchased from a qualified dealer
      Vehicle must not have already been claimed for a clean vehicle credit prior to this sale

To claim your tax credits, file Form 8936 with your tax return, including your vehicle's VIN and the sales report which you obtained during purchase from the seller and includes necessary vehicle and buyer information.

Charging Incentives/Range Anxiety

Bipartisan Infrastructure Law

  • $5 Billion for EV Charging Networks

    • Allocated to develop a national network of EV chargers along Alternative Fuel Corridors—designated highways with infrastructure to support alternative fuel vehicles (electric, hydrogen, propane, etc.).

    Focuses on enabling long-distance travel for EV drivers by ensuring reliable access to charging points along major routes.

  • $2.5 Billion Competitive Grant Program

    • Targets the buildout of all alternative fuel charging infrastructure, including EV chargers, beyond the highways covered by the $5 billion program.

    Ensures equitable distribution by requiring at least 50% of funding to be allocated to low-income and rural communities, addressing gaps in charging availability.

Inflation Reduction Act Section 30C

Offers a tax credit covering up to 30% of the cost for purchasing and installing EV charging stations and other alternative fuel infrastructure.

Residential installations can receive up to $1,000, while commercial installations are eligible for up to $100,000 per property. Visit the Argonne National Laboratory to learn more about eligibility requirements.

Includes bidirectional charging equipment (used for both charging vehicles and providing power back to the grid or home) as eligible for the credit.

Extended through December 31, 2032.

Visit the IRS website for more information on Section 30C.

 Charging Incentives/Range Anxiety

Multi Unit Dwelling (MUD) EV Charger Incentive: A grant program by NJBPU that aims to address the challenges of shared residential spaces, where installing chargers can be more complicated due to ownership, infrastructure, or parking lot constraints.

Specifically designed to increase EV charging availability in residential settings, especially for residents of apartments and condominiums who may lack access to private charging infrastructure.

Target Audience

  • Property Owners
  • Condominium Associations
  • Landlords

Eligible Locations

  • Apartment Complexes
  • Condominiums
  • Affordable Housing Developments

If approved, these grants are often tailored to offset the cost of wiring, hardware, and labor needed to install charging stations in shared or assigned parking spaces.
